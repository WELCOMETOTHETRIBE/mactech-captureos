# Positioning & Go-to-Market — MacTech CaptureOS

## The positioning statement

> **MacTech CaptureOS helps defense contractors identify, win, and stay eligible for federal work — capture intelligence, proposal automation, and CMMC readiness in one platform built by the team that uses it to win contracts themselves.**

What makes this statement work:
- Leads with the *business outcome* (identify, win, stay eligible), not the feature.
- "Stay eligible" is the CMMC hook without saying CMMC — broadens the reason to buy.
- "Built by the team that uses it" is the single thing no competitor can copy.
- "Defense contractors" instead of "government contractors" narrows us to the buyer persona we can serve best.

## What we are NOT

Reject these framings publicly and internally:
- "Compliance software" — commoditized, price-compressed, budget fights.
- "A SAM.gov scraper" — implies we're a commodity data pipe.
- "A BD dashboard" — dashboards don't produce outcomes.
- "AI-powered" as the lead descriptor — every tool is AI-powered now. Lead with the outcome, let AI be the how.

## Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs)

### ICP-A: Small SDVOSB prime, 5–50 employees, 1–3 active NAICS

**Who:** Typically a founder-CEO with a technical or operations background, wears the BD hat themselves, CMMC is a board-level conversation, pursuing 2–5 opportunities per quarter.

**Pain:** Can't afford GovWin IQ ($15k+/yr), has outgrown USASpending.gov, using spreadsheets and a SAM.gov saved search. Wastes time chasing the wrong opportunities. No structured capture process.

**Our sell:** "Replace your spreadsheets and stop missing Sources Sought. Score opportunities against your actual capabilities, not just your NAICS."

**Price point:** $599/mo Capture tier.

**Where they hang out:** LinkedIn DoD cyber / SDVOSB groups, Bunker Labs, local PTACs, industry day events, r/govcon.

### ICP-B: Mid-size DIB prime, 50–500 employees, CMMC L2 forced

**Who:** Has a capture manager or small BD team, uses GovWin or HigherGov, now scrambling for CMMC L2 certification by their contract deadline.

**Pain:** Compliance cost is spiking, CMMC consultants charge $50k–$200k engagements, existing tools don't connect compliance to capture.

**Our sell:** "Get audit-ready AND win more contracts from the same platform. We built this running our own CMMC program."

**Price point:** $1,499/mo Prime + $15k–$25k CMMC Readiness engagement + $1k/mo monitoring.

**Where they hang out:** NDIA events, AFCEA, industry association rosters, prime contractor subcontractor lists.

### ICP-C: Large prime needing teaming partners

**Who:** Capture teams at $100M+ primes looking to bring on small-business subs for set-aside pursuits.

**Pain:** Finding qualified, vetted small businesses is slow and ad hoc.

**Our sell:** Teaming partner marketplace — access to our curated small-business network. Enterprise tier + services.

**Price point:** $5k+/mo Enterprise + per-opportunity services.

## Competitive landscape

| Competitor | Price | Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Deltek GovWin IQ** | $15k–$40k/yr | Data depth, Deltek Costpoint integration | Old UX, no AI automation, overkill for small biz |
| **Bloomberg Government** | $6k–$15k/yr | Policy + news | Not capture-focused |
| **HigherGov** | $500–$2k/mo | Modern UX, growing | Shallow workflow, no CMMC |
| **GovTribe** | $100–$500/mo | Cheap, clean | Light intelligence, no full pipeline |
| **USASpending.gov** | Free | Ground truth for awards | Read-only, no workflow |
| **CMMC consultancies** | $50k–$200k project | Expert humans | Not productized, not integrated with capture |

**Our moat (in order of defensibility):**
1. **Founder credibility** — veteran-owned, SDVOSB, real DoD cyber practitioners. Customers can't buy this with money.
2. **Data × workflow integration** — competitors do one or the other, not both.
3. **AI-native compliance automation** — the compliance matrix auto-generator is a 20-hour-per-proposal saving; it pays for the tool by itself.
4. **SDVOSB / small-business tuning** — incumbents under-invest in small-biz features because small customers pay less. We make it our priority.

## Pricing philosophy

- **Anchor high on value, low on entry.** $199/mo Scout is aggressive by design — our job is to get teams using it, then upsell to Capture / Prime / Enterprise.
- **Services subsidize product.** Managed Capture and CMMC engagements carry 60%+ gross margin and fund the SaaS build. Year 1 services revenue should meaningfully exceed SaaS MRR.
- **No freemium.** Free users are an expense, not a pipeline. Instead: 14-day trial of Capture tier, credit card required.
- **Annual prepay = 2 months free.** Standard SaaS pattern; improves cash flow.

## Go-to-market motions (by phase)

### Phase 1 — MacTech wins (months 0–6)
- Tool is internal only. No marketing.
- Every MacTech contract win is a case study in the making.
- Starting inventory at month 6 should include 3–5 documented wins attributable to CaptureOS-surfaced opportunities.

### Phase 2 — SDVOSB cohort (months 6–12)
- Hand-pick 10 SDVOSB primes in adjacent-but-non-competing NAICS.
- Free beta for 3 months in exchange for weekly feedback calls.
- Convert ≥3 to paid ($299–$599/mo) by month 9.
- Recruit via: direct outreach (LinkedIn to SDVOSB CEOs), referrals from PTACs, Bunker Labs partnership pitch.

### Phase 3 — Public launch (months 12–18)
- Marketing site live with pricing, case studies, founder bios.
- Content strategy: 1 substantive GovCon tactics post per week (not AI-written fluff — real playbooks from MacTech wins).
- Paid acquisition: LinkedIn ads targeting CEO/BD Director/Capture Manager at small-to-mid DIB primes.
- Trade show presence: at minimum AFCEA DCOI, NDIA SO/LIC, GSA Expo.
- Affiliate / referral: 20% revenue share for first 12 months to existing customers who refer a paid signup.

### Phase 4 — Enterprise motion (Year 2+)
- Dedicated AE hired.
- SAML SSO, audit log export, custom contracting (MSA + DPA templates ready).
- Target: 3 enterprise logos at $120k+ ACV by end of Year 2.
