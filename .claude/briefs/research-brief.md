## 5. Inferred user & critical path
Same primary persona as pass 1 (4 MacTech founders, Patrick + James code-fluent, Brian + John episodic). The pass-2 ask is grounded in two specific moments in the daily critical path:

- **Moment A — list scan:** Patrick opens `/opportunities`. Today he sees a generic score-bucket strip (Top fit / Worth a look / Watch list / All) plus a gold Sweet spots toggle. He has TWO saved searches with very different intents: "RMF/ATO daily" (broad cyber lane) vs "UFGS 25 / FRCS Cyber" (high-moat narrow track). He has no fast switcher between them — he either types `?assigned_founder=patrick-caruso` (gives both), pastes a remembered saved-search ID into the URL (no UI for this exists), or visits Settings and reads the saved-search cards as informational text.

- **Moment B — single-opp decision:** John clicks into an opp that fired sweet-spot. He sees:
  1. PageHeader with score badge and NoticeType chip.
  2. Meta strip (posted/deadline/set-aside/notice-id).
  3. PursuitPanel — "Add to pipeline" button (post-decision).
  4. DrafterPanel — "Generate draft" / drafts list (post-decision).
  5. AskPanel — Ask Claude streaming UI (decision-adjacent but it's heavy chrome).
  6. Two-column main: Brief panel (left) / Cyber posture + Incumbent + Capability (right).
  7. Score breakdown (collapsed `<details>`).

He is not in the pipeline yet. He came to the page to decide whether to be. The pre-decision evidence — the brief, the cyber-fit verdict, the clause hits, the why-it-matters — is at #6, three scrolls down on a 1440×900 viewport with the right rail closed.

The fix is order, not content. Decision evidence top; do-work bottom; high-moat evidence inline above the brief when present.

- **Friction points observed in code (this pass specifically):**
  - The PursuitPanel renders before the brief panel even when the pursuit doesn't exist (`PursuitPanel` lines 477–506 — empty state with "Add to pipeline" button). Empty state of a do-work panel is the loudest pre-decision affordance on the page.
  - The high-moat block exists on the API response (`OpportunityDetail.score.high_moat`) but isn't even in the TypeScript type. Wiring it is a prerequisite for everything else.
  - The brief tab affordance is fake-tabs; we need real tabs (search-param-driven, server-component-friendly).
  - Saved searches have no list-page front door; they live only in the email-digest worker and as informational cards in settings.

Switching between perspectives today requires either editing the URL or going through the segmented score bucket + assigned-founder facet.
